Ethical Ramifications

While the Transcontinental Railroad brought economic growth and national unity, it also had significant negative consequences.

Negative Impacts

  • Ecological destruction - Railroad crews and commercial hunters used the new rail lines to kill millions of buffalo, collapsing the Plains ecosystem and devastating tribes like the Sioux and Cheyenne, who relied on them.
  • Forced assimilation - The railroad accelerated U.S. settlement, which led to Native children being taken to boarding schools and tribes being pushed onto reservations.
  • Mining and ranching expansion - The railroad opened the West to large‑scale mining and cattle ranching, which polluted rivers, overgrazed land, and displaced smaller Indigenous and Mexican communities.
  • Urban boom‑and‑bust cycles - Towns sprang up along the railroad, but many collapsed once construction moved on, leaving behind economic instability and abandoned settlements.
