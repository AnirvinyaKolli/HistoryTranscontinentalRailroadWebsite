Ethical Ramifications

While the Transcontinental Railroad brought economic growth and national unity, it also had significant negative consequences.

Impact on Native Americans

Construction and expansion led to the displacement of Native American tribes from their lands. The railroad disrupted traditional ways of life and contributed to violent conflicts.

Environmental Damage

The project caused widespread environmental disruption, including deforestation and habitat destruction. The near extinction of the American bison was accelerated by railroad access, which enabled mass hunting.

Labor Exploitation

Many workers, especially Chinese immigrants, faced dangerous conditions, low wages, and discrimination. Their contributions were essential but often overlooked and underappreciated.

Long-Term Effects

Although the railroad strengthened the nation and boosted the economy, it also raised ethical concerns about how progress was achieved— particularly regarding inequality, environmental harm, and the treatment of marginalized groups.
