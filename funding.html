Funding

Header

The construction of the Transcontinental Railroad was made possible through federal support, primarily the Pacific Railway Act of 1862. This act provided land grants and government bonds to private railroad companies, encouraging construction. Instead of cash payments, companies were given amounts of public land and financial bonds for each mile of track laid. This system motivated faster construction but also led to competition and questionable practices to maximize profits.

The three main companies involved were:

  • Central Pacific Railroad built eastward from California.
  • Union Pacific Railroad built westward from Nebraska.
  • Western Pacific Railroad Company in California.

The total cost of the railroad was estimated between $60–100 million in 1860s dollars, making it one of the largest infrastructure investments of its time.
