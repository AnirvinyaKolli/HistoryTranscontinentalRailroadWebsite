What was the first transcontinetal railroad?

The Transcontinental Railroad was a 1,911-mile rail line built between 1863 and 1869 that connected the eastern United States rail network at Council Bluffs, Iowa, to the Pacific Coast at Oakland, California. This monumental project drastically reduced country travel time from four to six months by wagon to about one week by train. The railroad was driven by the need to unify the nation both physically and politically, especially after the American Civil War. It connected eastern and western markets, encouraged trade, and supported westward expansion inspired by Manifest Destiny. Beyond transportation, the railroad became a symbol of national unity and economic growth. It opened new opportunities for settlement, business, and communication, fundamentally reshaping the United States into a more connected and industrialized nation.
